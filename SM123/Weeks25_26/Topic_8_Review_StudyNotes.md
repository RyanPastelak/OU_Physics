# Topic 8 Review

## Summary
This review consolidates the knowledge gained about the Solar System, focusing on bodies visited by spacecraft.
*   **Part 1:** Formation of the Solar System (gas and dust cloud), Kuiper Belt Objects (Pluto, Arrokoth), and Comets (Rosetta mission, dust analysis).
*   **Part 2:** The Asteroid Belt (Ceres, Vesta), meteorite classification (chondrites, achondrites), and their link to Solar System formation.
*   **Part 3:** Mars (landscapes, evidence of water, potential for life, Martian meteorites).
*   **Part 4:** Moons (Earth's Moon formation/composition, Jupiter's Europa, Saturn's Enceladus and Titan) and the search for habitability (subsurface oceans).

## Topic 8 Quiz Questions

**Question 1: Solar System Facts**
Identify the four true statements from the list regarding Solar System formation, location, and components (Comets, KBOs, etc.).

**Question 2: Comet Mass Calculation**
Calculate the mass of a spherical comet with radius $r = 1500$ m and density $\rho = 500$ kg m⁻³.
(Options provided in tonnes/kilotonnes).

**Question 3: Vesta Physics**
Given Vesta mass $M = 2.59 \times 10^{20}$ kg and radius $r = 253$ km:
a. Calculate acceleration due to gravity $g$ on the surface.
b. Calculate the weight of a 75.0 kg astronaut on Vesta.
c. Calculate the escape velocity from Vesta.

**Question 4: Meteorite Probability**
20,000 meteorites (>0.1 kg) land on Earth annually. Calculate the frequency of falls on Milton Keynes (Area = 89 km²). Assume Earth radius $r = 6370$ km.

**Question 5: Mars Matching**
Match objects/terms (Curiosity, Valles Marineris, Olympus Mons, Jezero, Nakhla) to their descriptions (Rover, Rift, Volcano, Delta, Meteorite).

**Question 6: Mars Rocks**
Describe the main rock type on Mars and its formation.

**Question 7: Moon Hazards**
Identify three hazards spacesuits shield astronauts from on the Moon (besides lack of air).

**Question 8: Moons True/False**
Identify true statements about the Moon's formation, Europa, Titan, Enceladus, and other solar system bodies.

**Question 9: Object Matching**
Match names (Churyumov–Gerasimenko, Europa, Charon, Chelyabinsk, Eris, Vesta, 'Oumuamua, Enceladus) to their type (Comet, Moon, Meteorite, etc.).
