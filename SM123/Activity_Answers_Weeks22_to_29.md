## Topic 7: Components of the Universe (Weeks 22-23)

### Part 1: Observing the Universe
**Activity 1.1: Scale of the Universe**
*   **Solar System:** ~40-50 AU radius.
*   **Nearest Star:** ~4.2 light-years.
*   **Milky Way Galaxy:** ~100,000 light-years diameter.
*   **Observable Universe:** Billions of light-years.

**Activity 1.2: Naked-eye Observation**
*   **Sun/Moon:** Obvious discs.
*   **Planets:** Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn (look like bright stars that don't twinkle).
*   **Stars:** Pinpoints of light.
*   **Milky Way:** Hazy band (if dark enough).
*   **Andromeda Galaxy:** Faint fuzz (very difficult).

**Activity 1.3: Distance to the Moon**
*   **Method:** Use a coin ($d_{coin}$) at distance $L$ to eclipse the Moon.
*   **Angular Size:** $\theta^\circ \approx 57.3 \times (d_{coin}/L)$. Typically $\approx 0.5^\circ$.
*   **Distance:** $D_{moon} = 3474 \text{ km} / (\theta_{rad})$. Result $\approx 384,000 \text{ km}$.

### Part 2: Planetary Systems
**Activity 2.1: Classification**
*   **Terrestrial:** Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars.
*   **Giant:** Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune.
*   **Dwarf:** Pluto, Ceres, Eris.

**Activity 2.2: Mass of Jupiter**
*   **Aim:** Determine $M_{Sun}/M_{Jup}$ ratio.
*   **Method:** Plot $P^2$ vs $a^3$ for Solar System planets and Jovian moons.
*   **Result:** The gradients differ by a factor of ~1000, showing the Sun is ~1000x more massive than Jupiter.

**Activity 2.4: Transit Investigation**
*   **Transit Depth:** $(R_p/R_*)^2$.
*   **Observations:** Larger planet $\to$ deeper transit. Larger star $\to$ shallower transit. Inclination must be $\approx 90^\circ$.

**Activity 2.5: Radial Velocity**
*   **Amplitude:** Increases with planet mass ($M_p$) and decreases with orbit size ($\sqrt{a}$).
*   **Inclination:** Max amplitude at $90^\circ$, zero at $0^\circ$ (face-on).

### Part 3: Galaxies
**Activity 3.1: Galaxy Shapes (Lemon Experiment)**
*   **Insight:** A 2D image is a projection. An "elliptical" shape could be a sphere, a flattened disk, or a cigar shape depending on viewing angle.

**Activity 3.2: Classification**
*   **Spiral:** Arms, dust lanes, young blue stars.
*   **Elliptical:** Featureless, smooth, old red/yellow stars.

**Activity 3.3: Orbital Speeds**
*   **Solar System:** Speed decreases with distance ($v \propto 1/\sqrt{r}$).
*   **Galaxies:** Speed is constant with distance (Flat Rotation Curve).
*   **Conclusion:** Galaxies contain huge amounts of invisible **Dark Matter**.

### Mathematics Practice: Orbits and Series
**Activity 2: Titius-Bode Predictions**
*   $n=-\infty \to 0.4$ AU (Mercury).
*   $n=0 \to 0.7$ AU (Venus).
*   $n=1 \to 1.0$ AU (Earth).
*   $n=2 \to 1.6$ AU (Mars).
*   $n=3 \to 2.8$ AU (Ceres/Asteroid Belt) - **Prediction verified**.

**Activity 5: Linearizing Dermott's Law**
*   **Law:** $T_n = T_0 A^n$.
*   **Linear Form:** $\log(T_n) = n \log(A) + \log(T_0)$.
*   **Plot:** $\log(T)$ vs $n$. Gradient gives $\log(A)$.

**Activity 8: Jupiter's Moons**
*   **Resonance:** Orbital periods of Io, Europa, Ganymede are in ratio 1:2:4.
*   **Geometric Series:** Common ratio $A \approx 2$.

## Topic 8: Exploring the Solar System (Weeks 25-26)

### Part 1: Pluto and Comets
**Activity 1.1: Cometary Dust**
*   **Method:** Measure crater sizes on impact foil.
*   **Relationship:** Crater diameter is proportional to particle diameter.

### Part 2: Asteroids
**Activity 2.1: Virtual Microscope - Meteorites**

*   **Carbonaceous Chondrite:** Dark matrix, white CAIs, chondrules. Primitive.
*   **Achondrite:** Interlocking crystals (igneous texture), no chondrules. Like terrestrial basalt.

### Part 3: Mars

## Topic 9: The Evolving Universe (Weeks 27-28)

### Part 1: The Expanding Universe
**Activity 1.1: Typical Sample**
*   **Concept:** The "10th brightest" galaxy is a standard candle.
*   **Analogy:** The 10th largest potato in a sack is a "standard potato".

**Activity 1.2: Virtual Telescope**
*   **Measurements:** Redshift ($z$) and Brightness ($F$).
*   **Calculations:** Speed $v = cz$, Distance $d = \sqrt{L/4\pi F}$.
*   **Result:** Plotting $v$ vs $d$ gives a straight line. Gradient is Hubble Constant $H_0 \approx 70 \text{ km s}^{-1} \text{ Mpc}^{-1}$.

### Part 2: The Cooling Universe
**Activity 2.1: Black-Body Spectra**
*   **Wien's Law:** Peak wavelength $\lambda_{peak} \propto 1/T$.
*   **3000 K:** Peak in Infrared (~1 $\mu$m).
*   **3 K:** Peak in Microwave (~1 mm).

### Part 3: The Evolving Universe
**Activity 3.1: History of the Universe**
*   **Sequence:**
    1.  Quark/Lepton formation.
    2.  Matter/Antimatter annihilation.
    3.  Proton/Neutron formation.
    4.  Nucleosynthesis (He, D, Li).
    5.  Recombination (Atoms form, CMB released).
    6.  Stars and Galaxies form.

### Mathematics Practice: The Scale Factor
**Activity 2: Hubble Parameter**
*   **Derivation:** Differentiating $D(t) \propto R(t)$ gives $v = (\dot{R}/R) D$. Hence $H = \dot{R}/R$.

**Activity 5: Redshift**
*   **Relationship:** $1+z = R(today) / R(emit)$.
*   **Example:** $z=1$ means the Universe was half its current size when light was emitted.

**Activity 7: Linear Approximation**
*   **Formula:** $z \approx H_0 (t_0 - t)$.
*   **Application:** For small look-back times, redshift is proportional to time (and distance).
