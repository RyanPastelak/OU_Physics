# Practical 2 Introduction: Observational Investigation

## Summary

This practical week focuses on the scientific process: planning and conducting investigations, analyzing and interpreting data, and communicating findings.

## Tasks Overview

| Part | Content | Time (hours) |
|------|---------|--------------|
| Part 1 | Thinking back and looking forward | 1.0 |
| Part 2 | Background radiation - what is it and how do we measure it? | 6.5 |

## Key Activities

1. **Reflect** on earlier practical work
2. **Carry out** an observational investigation using a cloud chamber
3. **Prepare** a short presentation to report findings
4. **Peer review** work done by other students

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this practical, you should be able to:
- Understand theory concerning natural radioactivity
- Use appropriate methods to select, analyze, graph and interpret data
- Prepare a brief presentation of your investigation
- Make, record and interpret observations accurately
- Reflect on knowledge and skills relating to practical science
