## Topic 1: Forces (Weeks 5-6)

### Part 1: Elastic Forces
**Activity 1.1: Investigating the properties of an elastic band**
*Aim:* To determine the spring constant ($k_s$) of an elastic band.
*Method:* Suspend a water bottle from an elastic band, vary the mass (water volume), and measure the extension.
*Analysis:* Plot Weight ($W=mg$) on y-axis vs Extension ($x$) on x-axis.
*Expected Result:* The gradient of the linear section equals the spring constant $k_s$. For a typical heavy-duty band, $k_s \approx 70 \text{ N m}^{-1}$.

### Part 2: Forces and Motion
**Activity 2.1: Exploring frictional forces**
1.  **Identify forces:** $mg$ = Weight, $F$ = Applied force, $N$ = Normal reaction force.
2.  **Normal reaction:** $N = mg$ (on horizontal surface).
3.  **Static friction:** If at rest with $F=100\text{N}$, Friction = $100\text{N}$ (opposite direction).
4.  **Force to start moving:** $F_{max} = \mu N = \mu mg$. For $\mu=0.5, m=100\text{kg} \implies F = 491\text{N}$.

**Activity 2.2: Measuring the coefficient of friction**
*Method:* Tilt a surface until an object (can of beans) just starts to slide. Measure angle $\theta$.
*Solution:* The coefficient of friction $\mu = \tan(\theta)$.
*Example:* If sliding starts at $25^\circ$, $\mu = \tan(25^\circ) = 0.47$.

### Part 3: Electromagnetic Forces
**Activity 3.1: Exploring Coulomb's law**
1.  **Tripling one charge:** Force triples (Factor of 3).
2.  **Doubling distance:** Force reduces to $1/4$ (Inverse square law).

### Mathematics Practice: Forces Around You
**Activity 1: Modelling Hooke's Law**
(a) Phenomenon: Stretching of elastic object under force.
(b) Model: $F = kx$ (Linear relationship).
(c) Predictions: Force required for specific extension; extension for specific force.
(d) Deviations: Elastic bands aren't perfectly linear (hysteresis, temperature, age).

**Activity 2: Linear Model from Data**
(a) Gradient $m = \frac{5.1 - 2.6}{0.060 - 0.029} \approx 81 \text{ N m}^{-1}$.
(b) Equation: $y - 5.1 = 81(x - 0.060) \implies y = 81x + 0.26$.
(c) Intercepts: x-intercept $\approx -0.0032\text{m}$, y-intercept $= 0.26\text{N}$.
(e) Prediction at 0N: Extension of $-0.0032\text{m}$ (physically unrealistic, limits of model).

**Activity 3: Simultaneous Equations**
(a) Equations: $5.1 = 0.060m + c$ and $2.6 = 0.029m + c$.
(b) Solution: Matches Activity 2 ($m \approx 81, c \approx 0.26$).

**Activity 4: Connected Masses**
(a) Free-body diagrams: Both have Tension $T$ up, Weight ($mg$) down.
(b) Equations: $T - 1.079 = 0.11a$ and $T - 1.472 = -0.15a$.
(c) Solution: $a \approx 1.51 \text{ m s}^{-2}$, $T \approx 1.25 \text{ N}$.

**Activity 5: Quadratic Coulomb**
(a) Quadratic: $F_{el}r^2 - k_eQ_1Q_2 = 0$.
(b) Distance $r$: Solving for $r$ gives $5.29 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$.
(c) Charges: Solving simultaneous equations gives $Q_1 = 10^{-5}\text{C}$ and $Q_2 = 1.11 \times 10^{-5}\text{C}$.

## Topic 2: Energy (Weeks 8-9)

### Part 1: The Energy Account
**Activity 1.1: Home Energy Categories**
- **Heat:** Washing machine, Kettle, TV, Fridge.
- **Light:** TV, Computer, Light bulb.
- **Sound:** TV, Radio.
- **Motion:** Washing machine, Vacuum, Fridge (compressor).

**Activity 1.2: Estimating Appliance Usage**
*Example:*
- Kettle (2.2 kW, 3 min): 0.11 kWh.
- Computer (300 W, 2 hrs): 0.6 kWh.
- Light (60 W, 5 hrs): 0.3 kWh.

**Activity 1.3: Measuring Consumption**
*Method:* Use electricity meter flashes/rotations.
*Example:* 31 flashes (1000 imp/kWh) in 41s $\implies P \approx 2.7 \text{ kW}$.

### Part 2: Kinetic and Potential Energy
**Activity 2.1: Kinetic Energy Examples**
- Spinning drum in washing machine.
- Flowing water from tap.
- Person walking.

**Activity 2.2: Planetary Orbits (Jupiter Example)**
- **Perihelion:** $E_g = -3.40 \times 10^{35}\text{J}$, $E_k = 1.79 \times 10^{35}\text{J}$.
- **Total Energy:** $-1.61 \times 10^{35}\text{J}$.
- *Observation:* Total energy is conserved (constant).

### Part 3: Work and Heat
**Activity 3.1: Heat Transfer Experiment**
*Observation:* Metals feel colder than wood at room temperature.
*Reason:* Metals have higher thermal conductivity, transferring heat away from your hand faster.

**Activity 3.2: Gas in a Box**
*Observations:*
- Higher Temp $\to$ Faster particles, harder collisions.
- Energy is conserved during collisions.
- Speed distribution: Bell curve (Maxwell-Boltzmann).

### Mathematics Practice: Energy Functions
**Activity 1: Energy Functions**
(a) $f_k(v) = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$, $f_g(h) = mgh$.
(b) Domains: $v \in [0, c]$, $h \in [0, \infty)$.
(d) Graphs: Parabola for $E_k$, Straight line for $E_g$.

**Activity 2: Inverse Functions**
(c) Inverses: $v = \sqrt{\frac{2E_k}{m}}$, $h = \frac{E_g}{mg}$.
(e) Speeds: $N_2 \approx 509 \text{ m/s}$, $O_2 \approx 477 \text{ m/s}$. Heavier molecules move slower at same temp.

**Activity 3: Reciprocal Potential**
(b) Function: $E_g(r) = -\frac{GMm}{r}$ (Scaled reciprocal).
(f) Negative scale factor flips graph: Energy increases (becomes less negative) as distance $r$ increases.

**Activity 1.3: Atomic Structure Summary**
- Nucleus: Protons (+), Neutrons (0).
- Shells: Electrons (-).

**Activity 1.4: Element Table**
*Key Examples:*
- Na: 11p, 12n, 11e.
- C: 6p, 6n, 6e.
- U: 92p, 146n, 92e.

**Activity 1.6: Orbital Configurations**
- N: $1s^2 2s^2 2p^3$ (2, 5)
- F: $1s^2 2s^2 2p^5$ (2, 7)
- Ne: $1s^2 2s^2 2p^6$ (2, 8)

### Part 2: Focusing on Metals
**Activity 2.1: Metals and Uses**
- **Steel (Fencing):** Strong, flexible, lightweight.
- **Aluminium (Food trays):** Good conductor, cheap, mouldable.

### Part 3: Ionic Interactions
**Activity 3.1: Building Ionic Compounds**
- Mg + F $\to$ Magnesium fluoride ($Mg^{2+}, F^-$).

**Activity 3.2: Writing Formulae**
(a) CaO (1:1)
(b) $Na_2O$ (2:1)
(c) $MgF_2$ (1:2)

**Activity 3.3: Ionic Bonding Revision**

- Non-metal gains e- $\to$ Negative anion.
- Held by electrostatic forces.

### Part 4: Covalent Bonding
**Activity 4.1: Lewis Structures**
1.  **Water ($H_2O$):** H-O-H (O has 2 lone pairs).
2.  **Sulfur dichloride ($SCl_2$):** Cl-S-Cl (S has 2 lone pairs).
3.  **Oxygen difluoride ($OF_2$):** F-O-F.

### Mathematics Practice: Density & Conductivity

**Activity 2: Density Calcs**

## Topic 6: Nuclei and Particles (Weeks 19-20)

### Part 1: Atoms and Energy Revisited
**Activity 1.1: Mass Numbers**
- H: 1, D: 2, He-4: 4, He-3: 3.

**Activity 1.2: Pair Production**
- Min Energy = $2 \times m_e c^2 \approx 1.02 \text{ MeV}$.

**Activity 1.3: Annihilation**
- Energy released = $2 \times m_p c^2 \approx 1.9 \text{ GeV}$.

**Activity 1.4: Uncertainty**
- Max lifetime $\Delta t \approx 4 \times 10^{-21} \text{ s}$.

### Part 2: Nuclear Physics
**Activity 2.1: Decay Fractions**
- 1 HL: 50%, 2 HL: 25%, ... 10 HL: ~0.1%.

**Activity 2.2: Beryllium Decay**
- After 6 half-lives ($82.8/13.8$): $1/64$ remains ($15.6 \text{ mg}$).

**Activity 2.3: Iodine Half-life**
- $20/640 = 1/32 = (1/2)^5$. 5 HL in 40 days $\to t_{1/2} = 8$ days.

**Activity 2.4: Sodium Decay**
- $1.67$ HL $\implies 14.2 \text{ g}$ remains.

**Activity 2.5: K-Ar Dating**
- Ratio 7 $\implies 3$ half-lives $\implies 3.75$ billion years.

**Activity 2.6: Alpha Energy**
- Energy = Binding Energy Difference = $4.9 \text{ MeV}$.

**Activity 2.7: Fission Products**
- Missing nucleus: Xenon-135 ($Z=54, A=135$).

**Activity 2.8/2.9: Triple Alpha**
- $3 \times ^4\text{He} \to ^{12}\text{C}$.
- Energy released: $7.3 \text{ MeV}$.

### Part 3: Particle Physics
**Activity 3.1: Antiproton**
- Composition: $\bar{u}\bar{u}\bar{d}$. Charge: $-1$.

**Activity 3.2: Neutron**
- Composition: $udd$. Charge: $0$.

**Activity 3.3: Pions**
- $\pi^+ (u\bar{d})$, $\pi^- (\bar{u}d)$, $\pi^0 (u\bar{u}/d\bar{d})$.

**Activity 3.4: Delta Baryon**
- $\pi^+ + p \to \Delta^{++}$ ($uuu$, charge +2).

**Activity 3.5: Collision Products**
- Various combinations conserving charge and baryon number (e.g., $n+n+\pi^0$).

**Activity 3.6: Colour Charge**
- (a) Red $\to$ Blue (after emitting Red-Antiblue).
- (b) Green $\to$ Blue (after absorbing Blue-Antigreen).

**Activity 3.7-3.9: Decay at Quark Level**
- Beta-minus: $d \to u + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e$.
- Beta-plus: $u \to d + e^+ + \nu_e$.
- Electron capture: $u + e^- \to d + \nu_e$.

**Activity 3.10: Neutron Survival**
- If $t_{1/2}=1s$, only $10^{14}$ neutrons remain after 3 mins (vs $10^{68}$ start).

### Mathematics Practice: Exponential Decay
**Activity 1: Exponential Basics**
- $f(x) = (1/2)^x = e^{-0.693x}$.

**Activity 3: Decay Parameters**
- Halving period equals half-life $t_{1/2}$.

**Activity 4: Time Forms**
- $P(t) = P_0 (1/2)^{t/t_{1/2}}$.

**Activity 6: Equivalence**
- $P(t) = P_0 e^{-kt}$ where $k = \ln(2)/t_{1/2}$.
